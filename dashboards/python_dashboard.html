Core Business Problem

A large-scale multi-region retail organization struggled with database performance bottlenecks and reporting discrepancies. Their transactional logging system recorded over 500,000 active records with multiple duplicates, blank entries, negative quantities (returns), and varying decimal precisions.

This portfolio showcase demonstrates a complete **Retail Sales ETL and Data Warehousing pipeline**. By migrating messy CSV records through Python-based Sanitization into a robust Star Schema model, we extract clean transactional records. Relational SQL queries are then used to monitor YoY sales trends, category profit margins, regional drivers, and product basket distributions.

Geographical Insights

North America ($5.8M Revenue)

Highest gross sales volume. High AOV ($38.50) driven by premium electronics category. Customer loyalty segment is exceptionally stable.

Europe ($4.2M Revenue)

Stable revenue patterns. Strong seasonal performance around winter holidays. Solid margins in the apparel sector.

Asia-Pacific ($2.9M Revenue)

Highest relative growth speed (+24.2% YoY). Heavy concentration of transient, first-time trial purchasers.

Latin America ($1.25M Revenue)

Underperforming sector. High product return rate (6.8%) leading to operational margin leakage. Requires marketing restructuring.

Immediate Retail Interventions

  • Home & Kitchen Pricing Restructuring: The Home & Kitchen category (accounting for 21% of revenue) suffers from a **-6.1% gross margin deficit** against our corporate average. Restructure localized pricing and vendor supply chains, targeting an average margin uplift of 3.5% through bulk purchasing agreements.
  • Latin America Logistics Re-Alignment: Latin America represents our highest regional refund drag, with a **6.8% return rate**. Audit localized logistics pipelines and implement strict parcel quality control, targeting a reduction of return rates to under 3.5% to save $45,000 annually.
